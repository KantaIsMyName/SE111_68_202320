A Brief Introduction/overview of the movie Puss In Boots: The Last Wish

The legend returns after 11 years in wait. Puss In Boots has been living the life of a legend. Traversing from town to town saving the day whenever and however he can so long as it involves a little showboating! But after losing his eighth life out of nine, Puss comes to face his own mortality.. and the potential of his own death. Watch as Puss In Boots, along with an old friend and some new, race to find the mythical last wish so that he can wish for his lives back and the legend can continue! But be warned! Not only legends have wishes and not everyone gets to see theirs come to fruition. Can Puss make his wish? Escape Death? And redeem himself?

My Review on Puss in Boots the Last Wish

While animation has been on the rise in the past few years, in terms of film quality animation we've seldom seen anything like this come from somewhere other than Japan. With recent hits like Demon Slayer: Mugen Train, One Piece Film Red, and Jujutsu Kaisen 0 it's a tall order to come fresh faced to the table with the first Shrek-universe film in 11 years. The film begins in classic Shrek fashion with the start of a fairytale, this time.. being the "wish upon a star" fairytale. It's a trope well played by the directors as this fairytale also plays as the main goal for the film, to recieve and make the one mythical wish. Now when being faced with such a polarizing character like Puss in Boots it can become hard to have the audience empathize with him, he's so grandeous and is larger than life in his delivery. That being said, having him die within the first 15 minutes of the film is not only hilarious, but reminds us (and puss) that he is mortal. It's at this point Puss learns he is on his last life, and as such must retire from being a hero. In his denile we are introduced to one of the absolute coldest villains to emerge from a dreamworks studio film ever. The big bad wolf. He instills a fear of mortality into puss and causes him to run. Through this series of events he meets a new friend (Perrito), reunites with an old flame (Kitty) and makes some new rivals and enemies (Goldilocks and the 3 bears, as well as Jack Horner respectively). The dynamic played between the many factions never feels dull or forced. There are parts of the movie where they have to interact with eachother that just come across as so human. One such instance is when Perrito is kidnapped by Goldilocks and the 3 bears. They have a roast session between Goldi and baby bear, which escalates to Perrito roasting the lot of them. After which they're all a bit more chummy together and Perrito tells Goldi that she's "Won the orphan lottery". Very human speech for an animated dog to be giving. But that's just a taste of the general dialogue offered in the film. Puss in Boots: The Last Wish does an amazing job at drawing out the empathy from it's audience and having it be shared amongst all the characters (Not Jack). If I had to give it a rating in relation to all film, it is a very solid 8/10. If I had to rate it only against other animated movies this is a clear 10/10 and should have taken the title of "Best Animated Film of 2022".

Puss in Boots the last wish Trivia!

  • Trivia #1: Despite featuring a predominantly latin/spanish cast and portraying the characters as such, Puss In Boots is actually an Italian fairytale!

  • Trivia #2: Antonio Banderas reprises his role as Puss in Boots in both the Latin American dub as well as the European Spanish dub. Oddly enough he doesn't reprise it in the Italian dub as he had since Shrek 3.

  • Trivia #3: This film marks John Mulaney's first role as a villanous character! He also performs the voice for younger Jack Horner in the film.

  • Trivia #4: The first Puss In Boots film came out in 2011 and work on this sequal began in 2014. 8 full years before the films eventual release!

  • Trivia #5: Antonio Banderas and Salma Hayek reprise their roles after 11 years away! Conrad Vernon and Cody Cameron also make cameos as Gingy and Pinnochio respectively! A rare feat for such a long break in voice acting!
